# OctoAcme — Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents. See [cadence and ownership](#cadence--ownership) below.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective
- Timebox: 45–75 minutes depending on team size
- Use an anonymous idea board if needed to encourage candor
- Prioritize 2–3 top action items to avoid overload

## Tracking Improvements
- Use the Retro Action Items template (`docs/templates/retro-action-items.md`) to record and assign action items during the retro.
- After the retro, **create a GitHub Issue** for each action item, label it `retro-action`, assign it to the named owner, and add it to the project board.
- PM reviews open `retro-action` issues at every weekly PM sync until closed.
- At the start of the next retro, review the status of previous action-item issues.

## Cadence & Ownership
- **Frequency**: After every sprint, major release, or significant incident.
- **Facilitator**: Rotating (default: PM schedules; any team member can facilitate).
- **Owner of follow-through**: PM ensures all action-item issues are assigned, visible on the project board, and reviewed weekly.
- **Review**: Action item status is checked at every weekly PM sync until all items are closed.

## Continuous Improvement Culture
- Measure impact of action items
- Celebrate improvements and make small, iterative changes
